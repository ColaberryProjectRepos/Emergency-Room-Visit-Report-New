Step 3 - Adding Additional Tables 12


Refresh Date Table

 

  • Open Power Query Editor:
    • On the Power BI Desktop ribbon, click on Transform data.
  • Create a New Blank Query:
    • Click on the New Source button.
    • Select Blank Query from the list.
    • Click on Connect.
  • Rename the Query:
    • Under the Properties pane, in the Name box, type 'Refresh Date'.
  • Enter DAX Formula:
    • In the DAX formula bar, type the following:
      = DateTime.LocalNow()
  • Convert to Table:
    • Click on the Convert to Table button.
  • Rename the Column:
    • When the Refresh Table pops up, right-click on the column header.
    • Choose Rename from the context menu.
    • Enter the desired column name that is "Date time".
  • Close and Apply:
    • Click on Close & Apply.
    • Save your work.

 

Calendar Table

 

  • Open Power BI Desktop:
    • Ensure that you have your Power BI Desktop open with the data model that contains the Patient_Dataset.
  • Open the DAX Formula Bar:
    • In the Modeling tab, click on New Table.
  • Enter the Calendar DAX Formula:

In the formula bar that appears, enter the following DAX formula:

Calendar =

ADDCOLUMNS(

    CALENDAR (

        Date(Year(Min('Hospital ER'[date])), Month(Min('Hospital ER'[date])), DAY(Min('Hospital ER'[date]))),

        Date(Year(Max('Hospital ER'[date])), Month(Max('Hospital ER'[date])), DAY(Max('Hospital ER'[date])))),

    "Month", FORMAT([date], "mmm"),

    "Weektype", IF(WEEKDAY([Date]) = 1 || WEEKDAY([Date]) = 7, "Weekend", "Weekday"),

    "Weekday", FORMAT([Date], "ddd"),

    "MonthNum", MONTH([Date]),

    "Month number", FORMAT ( [Date], "MM" ),

    "Month Year", FORMAT ( [Date], "MMMYYYY" ),

    "Week Number", "Week-" & WEEKNUM([Date]),

    "Week", WEEKNUM([Date]),

    "Day Of Month", MONTH([Date]) & "-" & FORMAT([Date], "dd"),

    "Year Quarter", FORMAT([Date], "YYYY") & "/Q" & FORMAT([Date], "Q"),

    "Year", FORMAT([Date], "YYYY")

)

Validate and Complete the Formula:

Ensure that there are no syntax errors in the formula.

Press Enter to create the new table.

  • Save Your Work:

Once the calendar table is created successfully, save your Power BI file.
